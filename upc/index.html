I.

The problem worth solving

UPC is excellent at selling prestige cars. What's missing is what happens after the sale.

Right now, when a customer drives off the lot, the relationship effectively ends. There's no system to remind them their Audi needs a service in 18 months. No anniversary touchpoint. No way to tell them when a newer version of their car arrives in your yard. Each customer is a one-time transaction.

Most dealers compete on the same axis: who can sell the next car to the next walk-in. The bigger opportunity is to compete on a different axis: who owns the post-sale relationship. Cars themselves come from Duttons. Leads come from CarSales. Neither of those is yours. The customer relationship is the one thing UPC can own outright.

The trade isn't between cars. It's between dealers. Whoever stays in the relationship wins the next sale. — The strategic core of this proposal
II.

What we're building

A single AI agent with four capabilities. Each capability takes care of a different moment in the customer's relationship with UPC. Together, they turn every car sale into years of structured engagement.

This isn't email automation. Automation makes you faster at the existing game. This is a coordination layer: it connects three things UPC already has (its customers, its inventory, and the passing of time) and acts on the patterns between them. The upgrade capability in particular makes real judgement calls about which past customer would be a fit for which current car, and what to say to them.

Service

Watches each customer's car and triggers a service reminder when one is due. Picks the right interval for the make and model (Audi service schedules differ from BMW from Porsche), and adjusts the tone of the reminder based on how long the customer has been with you. Outcome: workshop bookings UPC would have otherwise lost to whoever ranks first on Google.

Anniversary

Remembers the date each customer picked up their car. Triggers a personal note from Gino at the 12-month mark with a free wash offer. The kind of touch that big dealers don't bother with and that UPC has never had the systems to manage. Outcome: goodwill, reviews, referrals, and a returning customer who comes by the yard.

Upgrade The agentic one

Watches your inventory daily. When a new car arrives, the agent looks across your past customer base and asks: who would this car suit? It picks the right past customer, identifies the meaningful spec differences between their car and the new one, and drafts a personal note from Gino with the comparison built in. Gino reviews, edits if needed, sends with one tap. Outcome: repeat car sales. The capability that turns a one-time buyer into a returning customer over 3 to 5 years.

Learning

Captures Gino's edits and decisions. When he changes a phrase, deletes a spec point, or skips a customer, the agent notices. Over time, we use those patterns to tune what the agent suggests next, and to promote consistently-approved match types to send automatically without his review. Outcome: the agent gets sharper the more it's used, and Gino spends less time on it as it earns his trust.

The agent is one product, deployed in layers across six months. Each month it gains a new capability or matures an existing one. By month 6, the agent is operational across all four capabilities and learning continuously.
